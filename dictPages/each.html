The verb should still be singular: Each of these people has some useful talent or experience.• You usually use ‘his’, ‘her’, ‘himself’, or ‘herself’ after each: Each boy is issued with his own locker.Each of them tried it for herself.• However, you can use ‘they’, ‘them’, ‘their’ etc when you do not want to say whether people are male or female: Each individual has a right to express their views.• Don’t use each in negative clauses. Use none: None of the answers was correct. ✗Don’t say: Each of the answers was not correct.USAGE: Each, every• It is often correct to use either each or every, but they have slightly different meanings.• Use each when you are thinking about the people or things in a group separately, one by one: Each student came forward to receive a medal.Each time you exercise, you get a little stronger.• Use every when you are thinking about the whole group of people or things together, with no exceptions: Every student was given a prize.You have to enter your password every time you log on.• Don’t use each after words such as ‘almost’, ‘nearly’, or ‘not’.
