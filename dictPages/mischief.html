From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishmischiefmis‧chief /ˈmɪstʃɪf/ ●○○ noun    1 [uncountable]BEHAVE bad behaviour, especially by children, that causes trouble or damage, but no serious harm  Now run along, and don’t get into mischief.  They’ve got enough toys to keep them out of mischief for a while.  If you can’t see Nick, you can be sure he’s up to some mischief (=behaving badly and causing trouble or damage).2 [uncountable]TRICK/DECEIVE the pleasure or enjoyment of playing tricks on people or embarrassing them  Kiki’s eyes were bright with mischief.3 → make mischief4 → do yourself a mischief 5 [uncountable] formalDAMAGE damage or harm that is done to someone or to their property  The jury cleared him of the charge of criminal mischief.COLLOCATIONS – Meanings 1 &
