From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishdemolitiondem‧o‧li‧tion /ˌdeməˈlɪʃən/ ●○○ noun [countable, uncountable]    1 the complete destruction of a building  Consent is required for the demolition of any building.  Demolition work has already begun at the site.2 an easy defeat  50,000 fans watched the 7-0 demolition of Dynamo Kiev.3 a process in which something is ended or ruined completely  The policies are intended to halt the demolition of the rural economy. 4 the act of proving that an idea, opinion etc is wrong  This is a legend that deserves demolition.
