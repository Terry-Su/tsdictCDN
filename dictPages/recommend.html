something: I recommend that she speaks to a lawyer. ‘That’ can be omitted: I recommend she speaks to a lawyer.• In more formal English, you recommend that someone do something, using the base form of the verb (=the infinitive without ‘to’): I recommend that she speak to a lawyer. ✗Don’t say: I recommend her to speak to a lawyer.• In more formal English, you also use the base form of the verb when talking about the past: I recommended that she speak to a lawyer. • You recommend that someone should do something: I recommended that they should get insurance. • You can say it is recommended that someone (should) do something: It is recommended that everyone (should) take the test.
