From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishimitateim‧i‧tate /ˈɪmɪteɪt/ ●●○ verb [transitive]    1 IMITATEto copy the way someone behaves, speaks, moves etc, especially in order to make people laugh  She was a splendid mimic and loved to imitate Winston Churchill. ► Do not use imitate to mean ‘do the same thing as someone else’.
