From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishmoreovermore‧o‧ver /mɔːrˈəʊvə $ -ˈoʊvər/ ●●○ W2 adverb [sentence adverb] formal    AND/ALSOin addition – used to introduce information that adds to or supports what has previously been said  The rent is reasonable and, moreover, the location is perfect.  The source of the information is irrelevant. Moreover, the information need not be confidential.RegisterMoreover is very formal.
